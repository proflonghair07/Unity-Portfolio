About Me

My name is Murphy Vaughn, and I am a Nashville-based developer with three years of experience creating games in the Unity game engine. I currently have ten released games and countless other prototypes. My expertise primarily lies in 2D game development, although I also have experience in 3D game design. I possess a solid grasp of C# and a knack for quickly adapting to new tools, which allows me to prototype rapidly.

My journey in the world of programming began with web design, where I learned the fundamentals through JavaScript. This initial foray into programming eventually led me to explore C# and the Unity game engine. What started as a small side hobby quickly evolved into a full-blown passion as I discovered how satisfying and rewarding the marriage of various creative and technical aspects of game design can be.

My main passions are scripting, level design, and sound design, but there are few areas in the field that don't intrigue me. I'm excited to continue growing, learning, and creating in this constantly evolving field. If you're looking for a dedicated and passionate game developer, let's connect and create something amazing together.

Candidus Caves

Candidus Caves is a Metroidvania game with a 1-bit aesthetic, featuring three unlockable upgrade abilities. Players assume the role of Magnus, a young boy embarking on his inaugural cave expedition with his father upon reaching adulthood. However, upon arrival, the lift allowing access to the caves malfunctions, leaving Magnus with the vital task of collecting enough 'Reparare Rhodonite' to repair it and continue their journey.

In the development of this game, a variety of tools were employed. With a few exceptions, most of the game's art assets were sourced from asset packs on itch.io. To achieve a more consistent look, all these diverse assets were converted to a 1-bit style using a dithering tool created by TimHeiGames. Sprites were then recolored as necessary using Aseprite. Additionally, the splash screen logo animation and various effects were crafted using CodeManu's pixel art tools.

A downloadable version of this project can be found here. The project's GitHub repository is available here.

Bubble Paths

Bubble Paths is a tranquil underwater platformer that combines a light narrative with puzzle elements. The player plays as Emster, a seasoned diver who, against his better judgment and driven by desperation, embarks on another dive to secure much-needed funds.

The game's central mechanic is a unique limited breath system. Players must navigate their way by colliding with bubbles that follow predetermined paths to replenish their oxygen supply. Bubble Paths offers a collection of 8 compact single-screen puzzle levels.

A browser build of this project is available here, and it's GitHub repository is available here.

Kit Twins III

Kit Twins III was my submission to the 2023 GMTK Game Jam. As the title suggests, it is my third entry in this jam, with Kit Twins I being my first completed game. I decided to create a sequel to the first game during the second year because I thought it would be interesting to directly compare my first and most recent games, gauging how much I had improved. I have found this process enjoyable and plan to continue it in the foreseeable future.

The theme for this jam was 'Roles Reversed.' The central mechanic in all Kit Twin titles involves mirrored movement between the player and the active Kit Twin. The goal is to guide the twin to a campfire, allowing you to progress to the next area. For the 'Roles Reversed' theme, I had the platform container mimic the player's movement, with the player maneuvering it around the twin to reach the campfire. Each twin's platform container was created using Unity's tilemap system, and I was quite pleased with the results.

A browser build of this project can be found here.
